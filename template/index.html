Causes

Deforestation


Forests are carbon sinks because they take in CO2 from the air. But cutting down trees, mostly for farming and logging, reduces the Earth's ability to absorb CO2 and causes greenhouse gas levels to rise.

Industrial Activities


Industrial processes, like making cement, steel, and chemicals, release a lot of CO2 and other gases that trap heat in the atmosphere. Methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O) are two other powerful greenhouse gases that can be released by industries.

Transportation Usage


Transportation is a key part of getting people and things where they need to go. But most transportation uses fossil fuels, which when burned give off carbon dioxide and other gases that trap heat in the atmosphere. These emissions are a big part of the problem of global warming. Promoting greener options like electric cars and public transit, making fuel use more efficient, and investing in cleaner fuels can help reduce the damage transportation does to the environment and slow down climate change.

Greenhouse Effect


The greenhouse effect is when some gases in the Earth's atmosphere keep heat from the sun from escaping. These gases, like carbon dioxide and methane, take in infrared radiation and send it back out again. This keeps the radiation from going into space. This keeps the Earth warm enough to live on. But people's actions have caused greenhouse gas levels to rise, which has made the greenhouse effect stronger and led to global warming.
